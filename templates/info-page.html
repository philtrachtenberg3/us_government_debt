📘 What Is National Debt?

National debt is the total amount the U.S. federal government owes to creditors. It represents years of borrowing to fund wars, infrastructure, social programs, and bailouts.

“The fact that we don't know what debt is, and yet it's central to everything, is telling.”
David Graeber, Debt: The First 5,000 Years

Debt is not just an economic number — it's also a social and political structure. Graeber reminds us that debt reflects relationships, power, and historical context. The U.S. national debt isn't just a fiscal concern; it's tied to military spending, tax policy, and financial institutions.

💡 Did You Know?

  • The U.S. government has only been debt-free for one year: 1835 under Andrew Jackson.
  • Almost every modern country carries some form of national debt.
  • Wars — especially World Wars I & II — were major inflection points in debt expansion.
